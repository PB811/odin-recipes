Vada Pav

Ingredients

  • Potatoes (500 grams)
  • Oil (for deep frying)
  • Mustard seeds (1 teaspoon)
  • Curry leaves (8-10)
  • Green chilies (2)
  • Ginger (1 inch piece)
  • Garlic (6 cloves)
  • Turmeric powder (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Gram flour (200 grams)
  • Water (as needed)
  • Red chili powder (1 teaspoon)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Bread buns (pav) (4)
  • Green chutney (4 tablespoons)
  • Tamarind chutney (4 tablespoons)
  • Fried green chilies (4, optional)

Instructions

  1. Boil or steam the potatoes until tender. Peel and mash them well in a bowl.
  2. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. When they splutter, add curry leaves, asafoetida, crushed garlic, and chopped green chilies. Saut√© briefly.
  3. Add turmeric powder to the pan and stir for a few seconds.
  4. Pour this tempering into the mashed potatoes. Add chopped coriander leaves and salt. Mix everything thoroughly and shape into small to medium-sized balls, slightly flattening them.
  5. Prepare the batter by mixing gram flour (besan), turmeric, asafoetida, salt, and water to form a smooth, thick batter. Optionally, add a pinch of baking soda.
  6. Heat oil for deep frying in a kadai or deep pan.
  7. Dip each potato ball into the batter, coating it evenly, then carefully drop it into the hot oil.
  8. Fry the vadas on medium heat until they turn golden brown and crisp. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  9. Optionally, fry deseeded green chilies in the same oil for serving.
  10. Slice the pav buns horizontally without cutting all the way through.
  11. Spread green chutney on the inner sides of the pav. Optionally, add garlic chutney and tamarind chutney.
  12. Place a hot vada inside the pav, pressing gently to sandwich it.
  13. Serve the assembled vada pav hot with fried green chilies and chutneys on the side.

Enjoy your delicious Vada Pavs!
